## Proof of Main Theorem

> [!thm] Main Theorem    
> Let $L \in \mathbb{N}$, $p \in (0,1)$. Let $F$ be a DeMorgan formula with $L$ leaves and $\cR_p$ be the $p$-random restriction.
>     $$\Pr_{\rho \in \cR_p}[DT_{\text depth}(F|_\rho) \geq t] \leq (p\sqrt{L})^t$$
>


It is enough to bound the probability that $\Pr_{\rho \in \cR_p}[\CDT_{depth}(F|_{\rho}) = t]$ . 	
Since this probability goes down exponentially in $t$ and when summed over $u \ge t$, we get a geometric sum which sums to $O(p\sqrt{L})^t$. 

If $\CDT_{depth}(F|_\rho) = t$, then there exists a variables $x_{i_1}, \dots , x_{i_t}$ such that $\PV(F,\rho,a) = (x_{i_1},\dots ,x_{i_t})$ , where $x_{i_1}, \dots x_{i_t}$ is the sequence of variables encountered in the long path given by $a$ in $\CDT(F,\rho)$. 

$$\Pr_{\rho \in \cR_p}[\CDT_{depth}(F|_\rho) =t] \le \sum_{v_1, \dots , v_t} \Pr_{\rho \in R_p}[\PV(F,\rho,a) = (v_1, \dots ,v_t) \wedge v_1,\dots,v_t \in stars(\rho)]$$



If $\PV(F,\rho,a) = (x_{i_1}, \dots x_{i_t})$, by the Unpacking lemma, there exists $\Gamma, b^{\Gamma}$ such that $\Gamma$ and $b^\Gamma$ are recoverable from $F,\rho,a$. 

Unpacking says that if $\PV(F,\rho,a) = (x_{i_1}, \dots x_{i_t})$, then there exists $\Gamma$, such that $\cA$ holds. 

There is an algorithm which takes as input $F,\rho,a,\bar{x}\$ which satisfy $\cA$ and outputs  $\Gamma, b^\Gamma$.  








Furthermore, we can discover $\Gamma$ and $b^{\Gamma}$  from $F,\rho,a,\bar{x}$.

$\cA(F,\rho,a,\bar{x})= \Gamma$
 
$\Pr_{\rho \sim \cR_p}[\CDT_{depth}(F,\rho) =t] \leq \sum_{\Gamma, b^{\Gamma}}$
