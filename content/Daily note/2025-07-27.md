# Chronogram + Cell sampling + Peak to Average
$$\newcommand{\R}{\mathbb{R}}$$

>[!lemma] Peak to Average
> Let $f: \Sigma^n \to \R$ satisfying 
> 
> - $\exists x \in \Sigma^k$ $|f(x)| \geq \epsilon$;
> - $\sum_{x \in \Sigma^k} |f(x)| \leq 1$;
>   
> Then there exists $Y \subseteq [k]$, $|Y| \leq O(\sqrt{k \cdot \log 1/\epsilon})$ such that $\sum_{y \in \Sigma^Y}|\sum_{z|Y = y } f(z)| \geq exp(-\sqrt{k \cdot \log 1/\epsilon })$.

An indispensable ingredient of the proof is the usage of low-degree (multivariate) polynomials with “threshold”-like phenomena, commonly known as (discrete) Chebyshev polynomials. 
The lemma can be viewed as an interesting and efficient way of “decomposing” a distribution into a small number of conditional distributions, “boosting” the effect of a single desirable event, hence the Peak-to-Average Lemma may be of independent interest

Proof. 

- Assume $\Sigma$ is boolean.  
- Let $Q$ be the Chebychev Polynomial which $\epsilon$-approximates AND.
- $h(x) = Q(x)\cdot f(x)$ 

Consider $\sum_{x \in \{0,1\}^k} h(x) = \sum_{x\in \{0,1\}^k}\sum_{Y \subseteq [k], |Y| \leq d} \alpha_Y \prod_{i \in Y}x_i$

There exists $Q$(Chebychev Polynomials)
- $\#$ monomials in $Q = \binom{k}{\sqrt{k \log 1/\epsilon}} = O(\sqrt{\frac{k}{\log 1/\epsilon}})^{\sqrt{k \log 1/\epsilon}} = exp(\sqrt{{k \log 1/\epsilon}} (\log k - \log \log 1/\epsilon))$  
- $Q(0) \ge M$, $M = 2/\epsilon$
- $|Q(x)| \le 1$ for all $x \neq 0^n$.  
- degree of $Q \le O(\sqrt{k \log M})$ .
